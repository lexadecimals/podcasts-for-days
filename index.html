Podcasts for Days

A selection of my favourite podcasts

Creativity

Creative Pep Talk • Illustrator Andy J. Pizza is on a mission to help you fulfill your creative destiny. As well as putting a smile on your face, his weekly podcast will help you uncover your artistic blocks, leaving you eager to get to work.


Being Boss • Creative entrepreneurs Emily and Kathleen discuss a wide range of topics related to running a creative business.


Drunk on Lettering • Phoebe and Roxy (of San Diego Letters) have fun and informal chats (plus booze) with some of the best lettering artists around. Featured artists include Penmanships, Mary Kate McDevit and Jon Contino.


Creative South • Designer and host Jason Frostholm interviews designers, illustrators, photographers and creatives.

Soul

The Minimalists • For a brief moment in 2016 I had the grand idea that I would rid myself of all but my essential posessions. I would Marie Kondo through my stuff and wear the same outfits every day like John Luther and Martha Costello. In mid-2017 I find myself drowning in books and clothes I will never wear, but I still follow Joshua and Ryan's minimalst journey via their podcast, and highly suggest you do the same.


This Feels Terrible • The charming and relatable Erin McGathy has candid discussions about relationships, love and heartbreak, with a variety of interesting guests.


Another Round • Buzzfeed besties Heben & Tracy discuss politics, race, gender and popular culture over drinks.


Sleep With Me • Insomnia getting you down? Scooter, with his nonsensical storytelling and oddly relaxing voice, will have you catching up on all those z's in no time.


Just for Fun

Star Talk • Neil deGrasse Tyson hosts this fun podcast all about space, science, and popular culture.


The X-Files Files • Actor and nerd Kumail Nanjiani rewatches his very favourite show. Expect laughs and insight, and some great guests, including those from the The X-Files universe itself. Unfortunately, Kumail only gets through seasons 1-4 due to his acting career blowing up. On the plus side, this podcast led to him guest starring in The X-Files Season 10, Episode 3.


Welcome to Night Vale • Local weather reports, news, and important announcements from the sheriff's Special Police. Welcome to the weird and wonderful town of Night Vale.


Hall of Fame

Adam & Joe • 2006. XFM (back when women we're allowed to listen). Adam & Joe. A time when a podcast was a radio show with all the fun and laughter left in and all the annoying music/commercials cut out. If you want to laugh like a maniac when riding the bus or walking down the street alone, there are no subsitutes. Start with the early stuff on Soundcloud before making your way through the BBC 6 Music podcasts on iTunes. Then, should you feel moved to do so, shout "Stephen!" at the top of your lungs in a crowded place.


Serial: Season One • The podcasting world can be divided into pre and post Serial. With this weekly (serial) true crime mystery, we undoubtedly reached PEAK PODCAST. Even my mother listened, and she doens't even understand what a podcast is. Sidebar: I prefered "spin-off" S-Town to Serial's second season, and higly recommend it (the less you know going in, the better).


The Cecil Hotel • In this Halloween episode of Radio Free Burrito, podcast host and actor Wil Wheaton reads a haunting account of the 2013 dissapearance of Elisa Lam from the Cecil Hotel, California. The Cecil Hotel provided the inpsiration for American Horror Story: Hotel, and I listened this podcast hoping to be suitably spooked on Halloween. What I got was so much more. Wil Wheaton's voice is the perfect vessel for what is, ultimately, a heart-breaking tale.
